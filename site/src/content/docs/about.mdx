**CAAIL** (the Cellular Agriculture AI Library) is a curated, openly-licensed map of the work at
the intersection of **cellular agriculture** and **artificial intelligence** — the peer-reviewed
papers, open-source software, databases, and per-species datasets the field builds on.

## How it is organized

- **[Papers Explorer](/caail/papers/explorer/)** — primary research arranged as a matrix of AI
  method × research area, plus reviews and perspectives. Cells link to numbered references; each
  reference surfaces its DOI and any associated code or data.
- **[Software](/caail/software/)** and **[Databases](/caail/databases/)** — tools and lookup
  resources grouped by application area.
- **Datasets** — the train-on artifacts of the field, organized by species (cow, pig, chicken,
  fish, and more) alongside cross-species reference data and AI/ML benchmarks.
- **Research Areas** — deep-dive pages for media optimization, cellular engineering, bioprocess
  control, scaffolding, sensory prediction, and the AI tooling and evaluation that cut across them.
- **[Talks & Videos](/caail/talks/)** — curated lectures and explainers.

This site is a **navigation layer**. The canonical, GitHub-readable Markdown files remain the
source of truth, and every entry links back to its authoritative source — the curated summaries
here are deliberately compressed, so follow the links for schemas, licenses, and full detail.

## Contributing

Suggestions for new papers, tools, datasets, and resources are welcome. See
**[Contributing](/caail/contributing/)** for how entries are categorized and added, or open an
issue or pull request on the [GitHub repository](https://github.com/tucca-cellag/caail).

## How to cite

CAAIL is archived on Zenodo with a citable DOI
([10.5281/zenodo.20295590](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20295590)). If you reference the library
or build on its curated content, see **[How to Cite](/caail/cite/)** for the APA and BibTeX forms.
When citing a specific catalogued paper, tool, or dataset, cite the original source directly rather
than CAAIL.

## About TUCCA

CAAIL is a project of the **[Tufts University Center for Cellular Agriculture
(TUCCA)](https://cellularagriculture.tufts.edu/)** — an interdisciplinary center of excellence that
works to *"democratize the scientific building blocks of cultivated meat, build a pipeline of talent
and expertise, and foster public trust in this novel technology."* Its vision is *"a world where
everything from meat to milk to eggs can be grown from cells instead of animals."*

TUCCA is a collaborative hub spanning Tufts' five schools and eleven departments, and launched the
world's first undergraduate degree in cellular agriculture. Its
[research](https://cellularagriculture.tufts.edu/research) runs across the field — cells, media,
scaffolding, bioreactors and scale-up, media recovery, life-cycle and techno-economic analysis,
consumer assessment, and nutrition and safety. CAAIL grows out of TUCCA's **modeling and
informatics** work: the use of predictive, data-driven, and AI methods to accelerate every one of
those areas.

Learn more on the TUCCA [website](https://cellularagriculture.tufts.edu/),
[about page](https://cellularagriculture.tufts.edu/about), and
[mission, vision &amp; goals](https://cellularagriculture.tufts.edu/about/mission-vision-and-goals).
