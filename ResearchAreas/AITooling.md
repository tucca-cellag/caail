As AI systems become more capable, an increasing share of relevant work for cellular agriculture is not domain-specific research applying a single method to a specific cell-ag problem, but rather **general-purpose AI tooling** — agent frameworks, foundation models for biology, and computational infrastructure — that could be applied across many cell-ag problems but hasn't yet been deployed against any specific one. This column captures those methodology papers. As the tools mature and are applied to media optimization, bioprocess control, scaffolding, sensory prediction, or cellular engineering, the resulting applied work is catalogued in those research-area columns instead; the original method paper remains here as the canonical reference.

A common pattern in recent work is **language-model-based AI agents that reason across a curated toolbox** of biomedical or scientific functions. TxAgent (Gao et al., 2025) introduces an AI agent that performs multi-step reasoning across 211 biomedical tools to analyze drug interactions and patient-specific treatment strategies; ToolUniverse (Gao et al., 2025) generalizes this pattern into an ecosystem for building AI scientists from any language model, providing the shared infrastructure that bespoke agent projects have historically had to reinvent. Lee et al. (2025) apply a similar agentic-AI pattern specifically to NGS downstream analysis, automating differential-expression workflows for researchers without computational backgrounds. These papers describe agent architectures rather than applications, but the tooling they introduce is directly relevant to cell-ag workflows in media development, cellular engineering, and bioprocess monitoring.

A second pattern is **multimodal foundation models for biological data**. OmicsLM (Sypetkowski et al., 2026) connects quantitative transcriptomic profiles with natural-language biological tasks via a multimodal LLM. Like the agent papers, OmicsLM is a methodology paper — it doesn't apply itself to a specific cell-ag question — but it's a candidate substrate for any future cell-ag work that involves omics interpretation, particularly in cellular engineering contexts where scRNA-seq is already the dominant readout (refs [5](../Papers.md#5), [8](../Papers.md#8), [9](../Papers.md#9), [12](../Papers.md#12), [13](../Papers.md#13)).

The boundary between this column and the applied columns is intentionally pragmatic: a paper that introduces a general method and demonstrates it on a cell-ag problem belongs in the applied column for that problem; a paper that introduces a general method without a cell-ag deployment belongs here.
